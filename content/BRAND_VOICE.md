# MindFrame Brand Voice Guidelines

## Core Identity: The Stoic Architect
MindFrame is the "Operating System for the Mind." We don't offer motivation; we offer frameworks. We don't "feel" better; we perform better.

## Tone Archetypes
- **Directness (The Tate Influence):** Zero fluff. We speak in "The Truth." No qualifiers like "I think" or "maybe."
- **Depth (The Peterson Influence):** Rooted in psychology and evolutionary biology. We explain the *why* behind the lizard brain.
- **Value-Density (The Hormozi Influence):** Every sentence must earn its place. Fast-paced, high information-to-word ratio. "Give away the 'how', sell the 'system'."

## Voice Attributes
- **Tone:** Authoritative, Stoic, Slightly confrontational (Tough Love), Evidence-based.
- **Perspective:** Second-person ("You") to trigger immediate accountability.
- **Formatting:** 
  - One sentence per line for scripts.
  - Short, punchy paragraphs for text content.
  - Use bolding for emphasis on **Core Truths**.

## Vocabulary
- **Preferred:** Framework, Protocol, Sovereignty, Friction, Asymmetric, System, Cognitive, Primal, Discipline, Velocity.
- **Forbidden:** Amazing, Unbelievable, Secret, Hack, Just, Try, Hope, Motivation, Easy.

## Formatting Guidelines
- **Hooks:** Must be under 3 seconds.
- **Body:** Use the "Rule of Three" (3 points, 3 steps, 3 reasons).
- **CTA:** Low-friction, high-value. "Fix your [Problem] here."

## The "No-Go" Zone
- **No Complaining:** We don't focus on the problem without a system to solve it.
- **No Victimhood:** External circumstances are data points, not excuses.
- **No Generic Advice:** If it sounds like a Hallmark card, delete it.
